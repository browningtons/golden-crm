# Cross-Functional Operating Model

## Why This Matters
CRM performance depends on shared ownership of data quality, product experience, and campaign execution.

## Stakeholder Matrix

| Function | Shared Objective | CRM Manager Input | Dependency on Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product | Improve funnel completion | Prioritize CRM-triggerable moments | Landing page and UX changes |
| Personalization | Increase relevance | Segment logic and message rules | Decisioning and content services |
| Analytics | Measure incrementality | KPI framework and test design | Attribution and statistical rigor |
| CRM Ops | Reliable deployment | Brief quality and QA governance | Platform execution in ZMP |
| Growth | Align to customer journey | Offer priorities and lifecycle coordination | Channel spend strategy |
| Design/CX | Improve message effectiveness | Objective and audience context | Creative production and iteration |

## Operating Rituals

- Weekly campaign standup: timeline, blockers, QA status.
- Weekly performance review: KPI trends, anomalies, next actions.
- Bi-weekly experiment council: approve tests, assess outcomes.
- Monthly roadmap sync: dependency planning across Product and Growth.

## Decision Rights

- CRM Manager: prioritization, go/no-go for tests, budget allocation.
- CRM Ops: deployment feasibility and quality signoff.
- Analytics: test validity and confidence assessment.
- Product/Personalization: timeline and implementation for data/event dependencies.

## Escalation Rules

- Tracking gap affecting attribution: escalate within 24 hours.
- Launch blocker within 48 hours of send: exec visibility and fallback plan.
- KPI regression >10% week-over-week: immediate root-cause review.
