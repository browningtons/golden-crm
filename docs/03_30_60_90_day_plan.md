# 30-60-90 Day Plan (Manager, CRM Marketing)

## Days 1-30: Diagnose and Stabilize

### Goals
- Build trust with CRM Ops, Analytics, Product, Growth, and Creative.
- Audit lifecycle journeys, channel performance, data quality, and campaign calendar.
- Establish immediate fixes for execution reliability.

### Deliverables
- Current-state journey map with leak points and overlap.
- KPI baseline dashboard with last 2 quarters of trend data.
- QA checklist and launch readiness gate for all CRM deployments.
- Draft 90-day experiment backlog ranked by impact x effort.

### Success Signals
- Stakeholder alignment on top 3 growth opportunities.
- Reduced launch defects and clearer campaign ownership.
- Agreement on KPI definitions and weekly reporting cadence.

## Days 31-60: Launch High-Impact Improvements

### Goals
- Improve conversion in highest-value funnel stages.
- Establish testing system and standardized campaign briefing.
- Tighten channel orchestration and frequency management.

### Deliverables
- New triggered flows for top abandonment and re-engagement moments.
- First wave of 4-6 controlled experiments with holdout design.
- Standardized campaign brief process with CRM Specialist and Designer.
- Budget pacing model with monthly reallocation rules.

### Success Signals
- Early positive lift on conversion and engagement quality.
- Faster cycle time from brief to launch.
- Clear readouts with decisions after each experiment.

## Days 61-90: Scale and Institutionalize

### Goals
- Scale winners and sunset low-performing campaigns.
- Formalize cross-functional roadmap and governance.
- Define 12-month CRM roadmap tied to revenue targets.

### Deliverables
- Lifecycle playbook and channel policy document.
- Quarterly roadmap with test pipeline and dependency log.
- Executive-level WBR package linking channel metrics to revenue.
- Hiring/coaching plan for team growth and capability gaps.

### Success Signals
- Sustained lift on target KPIs with stable unsubscribe/complaint rates.
- Predictable campaign operations with fewer bottlenecks.
- Executive confidence in CRM as a measurable growth engine.
