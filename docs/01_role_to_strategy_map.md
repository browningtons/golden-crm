# Role-to-Strategy Map

## Objective
Convert the job description into a clear operating model that shows how the CRM Manager role drives acquisition, conversion, and retention outcomes.

## Responsibility Mapping

| Job Responsibility | What I Would Own | First Deliverable | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| CRM strategy across email/SMS/MMS/push | Lifecycle architecture, channel policies, segment strategy | 12-month CRM roadmap | Revenue per active user, conversion lift |
| Campaign planning and optimization | Automated + ad-hoc campaign calendar, cadence rules, creative testing | Quarterly campaign plan | Open/click/conversion rates, unsubscribe rate |
| Testing roadmap with CX/Product | Experiment backlog, holdouts, hypotheses, readouts | Prioritized testing board | Incremental conversion and lift confidence |
| Partner with Analytics + CRM Ops | KPI definitions, attribution, QA and deployment governance | KPI dictionary + QA checklist | On-time launches, tracking accuracy |
| Team leadership (Specialist + Designer) | Briefing quality, feedback loop, execution standards | Campaign brief template + review SLAs | Cycle time, rework rate |
| Budget and performance management | Channel budget pacing and ROI optimization | Monthly budget allocation model | CAC efficiency, marginal ROI |
| Product/Personalization collaboration | Event instrumentation and landing page alignment | Tracking dependency log | Event completeness, lower data loss |
| Growth collaboration | Journey alignment to offers and funnel priorities | Joint journey map | Funnel progression and conversion |

## Strategic Principles

1. Customer-first lifecycle design: message intent must align with funnel stage and customer readiness.
2. Test-learn discipline: run fewer, higher-quality experiments with clear statistical guardrails.
3. Channel orchestration over channel silos: coordinate timing and frequency caps across channels.
4. Measurable execution: every campaign has an explicit business KPI and instrumentation plan.
5. Operational reliability: strong QA and deployment process is a growth lever, not overhead.

## Monthly Operating Cadence

- Week 1: previous month performance readout, budget reallocation, experiment decisions
- Week 2: creative and messaging planning with Designer and CX
- Week 3: deployment, QA, and live optimization with CRM Ops
- Week 4: cross-functional roadmap review and next-month prioritization
